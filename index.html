Careers paths I've taken

I've pinballed between a couple different careers in 2 short years. I've gone from carpentry, to celltower technician, and currently trying to get into software development. I'm lucky to have been able to figure out what career I wanted while I'm young. At the same time I've been able to gain valuable experience in two vary different fields that most people wouldn't have.

Carpentry

I started an interest in carpentry after my basement had flooded and we re-did the floors. I had stopped doing any schoolwork entirely seeing it as unnecessary to the field, which was a bad decision. At 16 I had joined a vocational school for their building construction program. I was in the program for two years and received a heavy machinery certification and an OSHA 10 safety certification. After graduating I worked as a general laborer for 2 months at a historic renovation company in Saint Louis before being let go.

Tower Technician

After losing my job as a laborer, I called a buddy and had gotten a job as a celltower technician within the hour. I trained for three days on job safety and the equipment I would be using. The first couple weeks were spent on the ground, in a shelter, setting up the electrical and server side of the tower we were working on in Kansas City. I spent 3 days actually climbing a tower in Jeff City before realizing I was not cut out for the job's physical intensity.

Software Development

I decided to go to college for software development after climbing. I figured given my childhood around technology would help me flourish more in that environment than the other two. I moved to fenton and went to Saint Louis Community College for two semesters. once college became too expensive I moved back to Saint Clair and finally Sullivan to save some money for college. Eventually, I signed up for the LaunchCode class to try and jumpstart myself into the field, so I could afford to finish my degree in night classes.
